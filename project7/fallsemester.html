FYP IN THE FALL

The fall semester sets the stage for everything First Year Players does throughout the year, serving as the foundation for both personal growth and creative collaboration. It’s a time for new members to meet one another, build lasting friendships, and begin developing the skills that will carry them through the spring musical process. Through meetings, workshops, and social events, first years are introduced to the unique culture of FYP—one rooted in creativity, inclusivity, and support. By the end of the fall, members feel confident, connected, and ready to take on the exciting challenges ahead as part of the FYP family.

What Happens in the Fall?

Staff Retreat

Early in the semester, the FYP staff gathers for a retreat designed to build strong connections among members while laying the groundwork for the year ahead. Through team-building activities, leadership workshops, and collaborative planning sessions, first years begin forming relationships with one another and with returning staff. The retreat helps establish a supportive environment where members feel comfortable, confident, and excited to contribute.

Workshops

Throughout the fall semester, FYP hosts a variety of theatre and performance workshops aimed at helping first years grow both creatively and personally. These workshops focus on skills such as acting, singing, movement, and audition preparation, while also encouraging collaboration and self-expression. By participating, members gain confidence in their abilities and feel more prepared for future performance opportunities.

Fall Cabaret

The Fall Cabaret is a musical theatre showcase featuring performances by Syracuse University students across all majors and experience levels. This event allows first years to perform in a low-pressure, supportive setting while gaining valuable stage experience. Proceeds from the cabaret benefit Front Row Players, reinforcing FYP’s commitment to community engagement and using the arts to give back.

Mentor/Mentee Program

Each first-year member is paired with an upperclass staffer through FYP’s Mentor/Mentee Program, providing guidance and support throughout the year. Mentors help answer questions, offer advice on auditions and college life, and serve as a reliable resource within the organization. This program strengthens the sense of community within FYP and ensures that first years feel welcomed, supported, and connected from the start.
